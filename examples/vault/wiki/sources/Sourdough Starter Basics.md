# Sourdough Starter Basics

**Raw:** `sources/articles/Sourdough Starter Basics.md`

## Summary

A practical primer on creating and maintaining a sourdough starter — a live
culture of wild yeast and lactic acid bacteria in flour and water. Covers the
initial build (equal weights flour and water, daily discard-and-feed), a
maintenance rhythm for room temperature versus refrigerated storage, and how to
tell a hungry starter from a spoiled one.

## Key claims / techniques

- Equal weights of flour and water, fed daily with a discard step, stabilize into a usable culture in one to two weeks.
- Readiness test: the starter doubles in volume within four to eight hours of a feeding.
- "Hooch" (gray liquid) signals hunger, not spoilage; pink or orange streaks mean discard and restart.
- Wild yeast is most active around 24-27 C, so warmth fixes most slow starters.
