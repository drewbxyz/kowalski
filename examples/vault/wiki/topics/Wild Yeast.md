# Wild Yeast

Naturally occurring yeasts present on grain, in flour, and in the environment.
Together with lactic acid bacteria they form the culture in a [[Sourdough Starter]],
producing both leavening (carbon dioxide) and flavor (organic acids).

## Behavior

- Most active around 24-27 C (75-80 F); activity drops off sharply when cold, which is why refrigerated cultures only need weekly feedings.
- Feeds on the sugars in fresh flour, which is why a regular feeding schedule matters more than any particular flour brand.
- Coexists with lactic acid bacteria in a stable equilibrium once a culture matures — the acidity they produce keeps most spoilage organisms out.
