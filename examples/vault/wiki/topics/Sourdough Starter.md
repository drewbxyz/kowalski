# Sourdough Starter

A live flour-and-water culture of [[Wild Yeast]] and lactic acid bacteria,
maintained by regular feedings and used to leaven bread without commercial yeast.

## Building one

Mix equal weights of whole-grain flour and water, then discard half and feed
daily. Bubbles show up in two or three days; a dependable rise-and-fall rhythm
takes one to two weeks.

## Maintenance

- Room temperature: feed daily.
- Refrigerated: feed roughly weekly.
- Readiness test: doubles in volume within four to eight hours of a feeding.
- The daily discard keeps acidity in check — skipping it makes the culture sluggish and overly sour.

## Troubleshooting

Gray liquid on top ("hooch") means the starter is hungry: pour it off or stir it
in, then feed. Pink or orange streaks mean spoilage — discard and restart. Slow
starters usually need warmth (see [[Wild Yeast]] for the temperature range), not
a new recipe.
